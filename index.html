About Project HOPe

Addressing Youth Unemployment Through Innovation

Project HOPe is a partnership initiative between EcoRabbits Revolution and North Farms designed to tackle the 19.7% youth unemployment challenge, particularly acute in the Upper West Region where limited opportunities and climate vulnerabilities affect rural communities.

We aim to train and equip 100 youth in Year 1, with each doe expected to produce 20-30 kits annually. At GHS 150 per rabbit, beneficiaries can potentially generate substantial gross revenue and reinvest in expansion.

üìä Market Opportunity

Ghana imports over $300 million worth of meat annually. Local rabbit production can capture this market by offering affordable, high-protein meat to urban consumers.

üê∞ Why Rabbits Work

Rabbits reproduce quickly, require minimal land, have low startup costs, and integrate seamlessly into homestead systems for diversified income and risk reduction.

Our Approach

Comprehensive Training & Support System

Project HOPe will deliver practical training, starter inputs, technical mentorship, cooperative formation, and market linkages to create resilient, youth-led agribusinesses.

üéì

Intensive Training Program

Planned intensive curriculum covering animal housing, biosecurity, reproductive management, feeding, disease recognition, record keeping, business planning, and market negotiation techniques.

üì¶

Complete Starter Kits

Each beneficiary will receive: all-wire 6-room cage, breeding pair (1 buck + 1 doe), 10kg formulated grower feed, and hay bale designed for quick production cycles.

ü§ù

Ongoing Mentorship

Planned weekly remote follow-ups, monthly on-site visits, veterinary support, and cooperative formation to enable bulk procurement and coordinated marketing.

üè™

Market Linkages

Cooperative formation, buyer introductions, and supply contracts with hotels, restaurants, and retail outlets.

Implementation

Our Proven 4-Phase Approach

Phase 0: Foundation

Stakeholder engagement, baseline surveys, and procurement of training materials and starter kit components.

Phase 1: Training

Intensive 10-day practical training workshops with hands-on demonstrations and cage-building practicum.

Phase 2: Setup

Starter kit distribution and comprehensive on-site setup support to ensure proper facility establishment.

Phase 3: Growth

Ongoing mentorship, veterinary support, cooperative formation, and market linkage development.

Project HOPe Results

Expected Outcomes and Impact

Project HOPe aims to demonstrate the transformative potential of targeted agricultural interventions. Conservative estimates show significant revenue potential with built-in sustainability mechanisms.

Year 1 Employment Target

Aims to establish 100 youth-led rabbit enterprises with comprehensive training, starter kits, and ongoing support to ensure sustainable business operations.

Revenue Generation Potential

Targets approximately 3,000 rabbits to be produced in Year 1. At GHS 150 per rabbit, potential gross revenue of GHS 450,000 across all beneficiaries.

Sustainability & Scale Goals

Plans for revolving stock system, modest registration fees, and cooperative savings to ensure program sustainability while scaling to 1,000 youth over three years.
